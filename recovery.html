What recovery is

Recovery is the process of helping the body restore balance after stress, training, tension, inactivity, or repetitive movement. It can involve methods that improve circulation, tissue glide, mobility, and how the body feels overall.

  • Supports the body after training, work, or daily wear and tear
  • Helps reduce the feeling of tightness, stiffness, and restriction
  • Improves how muscles, fascia, and joints move together
  • Can support comfort, movement quality, and long-term consistency
Recovery is not just something you do after things go wrong. It is something you use to help the body stay functional, adaptable, and prepared.

How recovery works

1. Reduce tension

Recovery methods can help decrease the feeling of tightness and reduce stress in tissues that feel overworked, stiff, or restricted.

2. Improve circulation

Better circulation helps bring blood flow to an area, which supports tissue health and how the body responds after effort or strain.

3. Restore movement

Recovery can help tissues glide better and joints move more comfortably, which supports better mobility and overall movement quality.

Simple idea: less restriction + better flow + better movement = a body that feels and functions better.
